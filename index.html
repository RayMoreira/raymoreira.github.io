About

My path has not been a straight line. I trained as a physical therapist, earned an MSc in Biomedical Sciences and a PhD in Biotechnology, and spent years validating AI-based clinical assessment tools and teaching applied biostatistics and research methodology. Along the way, one thread stayed constant: using data to make health care more accurate, accessible, and evidence-based.

Today I work at the intersection of biostatistics, machine learning, and health research, writing statistical analysis plans, building predictive models on real-world clinical data, and communicating findings to clinical, technical, and non-technical audiences alike. I care as much about rigor and reproducibility as I do about impact.

I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Georgia Institute of Technology, working on digital health and assistive-technology research.
